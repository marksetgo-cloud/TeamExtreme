The Distinguished Members of TeamExtreme

A club of rare distinction, exacting standards, and a most selective membership

The Members Club

The Members Club positioning leans into the exclusive, time-honored tradition of our annual ski gatherings. This approach channels the legacy of distinguished alpine clubs and private ski societies, evoking the storied history of European mountain lodges and members-only resorts that have defined alpine culture for generations.

"Members Club" carries an inherent sense of belonging and privilege—not in an exclusionary sense, but in the warmth of shared tradition and earned camaraderie. It speaks to the bonds forged through our professional past and strengthened on mountain slopes each year. This is the nomenclature of establishments that have stood the test of time, where membership represents not just access, but kinship.

This positioning emphasizes the enduring nature of our friendships and the ritualistic aspect of our annual tradition. It suggests refinement, legacy, and the kind of sophisticated leisure that comes from deep professional success and cultivated taste. The Members Club framing adds gravitas and permanence—this is not a fleeting gathering, but an institution unto itself.

It's not just a group of people or a collective. It's a way of life. It's a club, it's exclusive. It's a philosophy.

Mark Woolley

Co-Founder & Chief Alpine Strategist

Mark co-conceived TeamExtreme during what can only be described as an extended philosophical discussion about the finer things in life. What began as joint speculation about creating an exclusive mountain retreat has evolved into a meticulously organized annual tradition, with Mark serving as the principal architect of its operational framework.

His leadership style combines the precision of someone who actually enjoys creating spreadsheets with an unwavering commitment to the pursuit of perfectly prepared venison. On the mountain, he approaches skiing with the same analytical rigor applied to everything else, favoring routes that maximize enjoyment while optimizing for risk-adjusted returns. He has been known to calculate the Sharpe ratio of various descent options before committing to a line.

Allegedly retired two years ago and appears to have been spending most of his free time on the slopes and the tennis court.

Bryan Smith

Co-Founder & Chief Philosophy Officer

Bryan's vision for TeamExtreme emerged from a simple but profound realization: that the finest things in life (the finest cuts of wild game, luxury accommodations, and the pretense of billionaire leisure) should not be occasional indulgences but annual obligations.

As co-architect of this distinguished institution, Bryan maintains an unshakeable belief that true alpine sophistication requires both technical skiing ability and the capacity to discuss cheese with appropriate gravitas. A devoted Shibumi disciple, he approaches the mountain with refined minimalism, which manifests in his principled refusal to use safety equipment.

Bryan's leadership style reflects his deep commitment to logic and the alignment of incentives. While governance matters greatly to him, he exercises influence quietly, speaking only after all others have been heard. This patient approach is balanced by his more conspicuous passion for fine colognes and his well-documented habit of acquiring aromatic candles at every destination, a practice The Committee has diplomatically described as "comprehensive olfactory curation."

Elsie Woolley

Vice President of Après-Ski & Dining Operations

Elsie joined TeamExtreme with an impressive résumé that included "professional enjoyer of mountain lodge atmospheres" and "certified expert in post-ski luxury procurement". Her approach to alpine adventure emphasizes the finer things: Fusalp's latest collections appear in her wardrobe with remarkable frequency, her handbag collection could warrant its own insurance policy, and her enthusiasm for raclette borders on the scholarly.

While other members obsess over vertical feet and powder conditions, Elsie maintains detailed notes on every boutique, restaurant, and raclette establishment within a 50-mile radius of our destinations. Her dinner reservations are secured with the tactical precision of a military operation, and her ability to identify the season's must-have accessories from across a crowded lodge is nothing short of remarkable.

Christy Smith

Vice President of Slope Operations & Sophisticated Living

Christy serves as Vice President of Slope Operations & Sophisticated Living with a sophisticated understanding that TeamExtreme's success depends equally on excellent skiing and excellent dining. She has developed what the organization considers an ideal balance of athletic ambition and culinary appreciation.

Her fearless approach to the mountain is legendary within the organization. She possesses an unwavering ability to fall and bounce back with remarkable resilience, which she demonstrates with impressive frequency. While she consistently refuses to let minor incidents diminish her enthusiasm, she is occasionally, but only occasionally, princess enough to require helicopter evacuation off the slopes instead of skiing down. These rare moments are viewed by The Committee as strategic decision-making rather than weakness.

She maintains that if one is going to pretend to be a billionaire, one should do so with appropriate commitment to detail.

Erik Greupner

Member Elect

Erik has recently become a member-elect of TeamExtreme, a designation that grants him all the responsibilities of full membership while maintaining an appropriate period of probationary observation. His initiation will include extensive education in the club's core values: venison preparation techniques, the proper appreciation of Swiss Rösti, and the art of appearing casually wealthy.

His prior legal training could prove unexpectedly valuable, particularly for emergencies of all kinds, including après-ski incidents that may require careful diplomatic resolution. Whilst he is accustomed to being the main man in the clubhouse, his desire to enter this members' club has necessitated a transition to understated excellence, a challenge he has embraced with appropriate seriousness.

His path to full membership appears promising, pending successful completion of the traditional Final Night Ceremony.

Laura Greupner

Laura has recently joined TeamExtreme as a member-elect, bringing with her a refreshing energy and what can only be described as an admirably pragmatic approach to probationary membership. While other candidates might attempt elaborate demonstrations of alpine expertise, Laura has wisely chosen to observe the proceedings with bemused tolerance.

As we look toward future expeditions, The Committee has quietly acknowledged that her expertise in the art of sophisticated hospitality may prove essential to our operational excellence .

Her path to full membership appears secure, pending the traditional elevation ceremony scheduled for the final evening of Sun Valley 2026.
